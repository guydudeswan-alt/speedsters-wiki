In the Arrowverse, the cosmos is bound by primal cosmic forces. The narrative centers on individuals who tap into these extra-dimensional energy fields, granting them god-like velocity. While heroic speedsters run to protect timelines and save multiverses, villainous speedsters weaponize their momentum to rewrite history, conquer worlds, and escape death. This eternal race alters reality itself, creating a fragile balance between absolute light and corrupting darkness. The grand conflict reaches its ultimate climax in the series finale, "A New World, Part Four," where the forces of light and dark battle one last time to decide the fate of the timeline.

Barry Allen (The Flash)

Bartholomew Henry "Barry" Allen is a forensic scientist who gained superhuman speed after being struck by lightning infused with dark matter during the S.T.A.R. Labs particle accelerator explosion.

Deep Lore & Powers

Barry is not just a user of the Speed Force; he is its generator and its soul. When he was struck by the dark-matter lightning in 2013, he became the literal engine of the positive Speed Force. Over nine seasons, his powers evolved from simple running to phasing through solid matter, throwing lightning, and manipulating time. His ultimate strength is not his speed, but his emotional resilience and refusal to kill, choosing hope over vengeance.

Series Finale Arc

In the final episode, Barry faces the ultimate speedster war brought on by Cobalt Blue (Eddie Thawne). Instead of fighting to the death, Barry chooses to break the cycle of violence. He tracks Eddie into the Negative Speed Force dimension and uses a heart-to-heart talk to convince him that the positive and negative forces must live in a balanced cycle. Eddie accepts, ending the conflict peacefully. Afterward, Iris gives birth to their daughter Nora, and Barry releases a massive bolt of lightning into Central City, sharing his powers with Avery Ho, Max Mercury, and Jess Chambers to create a lasting legacy of protection.
